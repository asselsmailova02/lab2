    • General information

    Zhanaarka District (Kazakh: Жаңаарқа ауданы, Jañaarqa audany) is a district of Ulytau Region in central Kazakhstan. The administrative center of the district is the settlement of Atasu.[3] Population: 32,312 (2013 estimate);[2] 30,818 (2009 Census results);[4] 32,184 (1999 Census results).


    History

    In the spring of 1941, Atasu received the status of a workers' settlement.In 1999, the population of the village was 14,281 people (6864 men and 7417 women). According to the 2009 census, 14,265 people (6958 men and 7307 women) lived in the village. At the beginning of 2019, the population of the village was 17,559 people (8689 men and 8870 women)


    Iron ore mining

    Extraction of iron ore for Karaganda metallurgical plants. As well as the commissioning of the Atasu-Alashankou oil pipeline became one of the most important events of 2006. The Atasu (Kazakhstan) - Alashankou (China) oil pipeline construction project is designed to transport oil from Atasu (Kazakhstan) to Alashankou (China), with a total length of 965.1 km and a diameter of 813 mm. The initial capacity of the pipeline is 10 million tons/year. The oil pipeline includes: GNPS Atasu, KUUN Alashankou.
